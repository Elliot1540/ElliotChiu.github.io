This project explores how handheld augmented reality can be designed for older adults in ways that feel usable, legible, and genuinely worthwhile. It considers common challenges in mobile AR, including screen readability, gesture complexity, camera movement, fatigue, spatial understanding, and the cognitive load created when digital content is layered onto physical surroundings.

The project frames older adults not as passive recipients of simplified technology, but as users with rich life experience, situated needs, and different forms of digital confidence. Through inclusive design thinking, it examines how handheld AR can support learning, memory, cultural engagement, navigation, and social connection while remaining respectful of comfort, autonomy, and accessibility.
