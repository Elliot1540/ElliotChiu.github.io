This project explores augmented reality as a medium for museum interpretation, with a focus on helping visitors encounter Rewi Alley-related heritage through situated digital experiences. The concept uses mobile AR to layer narrative, archival, visual, and contextual information into the physical museum environment, creating a more active relationship between visitors, objects, and historical space.

The project is also interested in how AR can support cross-cultural interpretation, particularly where museum materials connect New Zealand, China, education, social history, and international exchange. Rather than treating AR as decoration, the work positions it as a storytelling interface that can guide attention, reveal context, and make museum visits more memorable and accessible.
